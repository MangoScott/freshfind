freshfind

An AI tool that helps people find the food assistance they already qualify for.

The Problem

Millions of Americans qualify for food assistance but never receive it — not because they don't need help, but because the system is too hard to navigate.

42M

$12B

eligible people never apply

The barriers aren't about awareness — they're about complexity. The average person must navigate 30+ page applications, in-person interviews, and shifting eligibility rules that vary by state and county. For rural communities without broadband or bilingual residents facing language barriers, the process is even harder.

The Solution

FreshFind is an AI-powered platform that connects people with the food assistance programs they're eligible for — instantly, in plain language, in their community.

It works in four layers:

Layer 1
Am I eligible?
Answers in 30 seconds, not 30 pages. Checks SNAP, WIC, food pantries, and local programs simultaneously.
Layer 2
Where's the food?
Maps every food resource nearby — not just grocery stores, but pantries, farmers markets, and SNAP retailers that carry real food.
Layer 3
Help me apply
Walks through the actual application process step by step. Pre-fills where possible. Explains what documents you need and why.
Layer 4
Keep me enrolled
Monitors policy changes and recertification deadlines. Alerts users before they lose benefits, not after.
The Research

In collaboration with the University of Virginia, we conducted field research across five diverse communities — from Chicago's South Side to rural Appalachian Kentucky — mapping the actual on-the-ground barriers to food access.

Each community revealed different challenges but the same core finding: the programs exist, the money exists, but the navigation layer between people and help is broken. FreshFind is that layer.

The Impact
Every $1 of SNAP benefits generates $1.54 in local GDP. When we help one family enroll, we aren't just feeding them — we're putting money into the local economy. FreshFind turns government investment into community impact.

The platform is designed as a replicable toolkit, not a custom build. Any state SNAP agency, county health department, or 211 system can deploy it by supplying their local data. At the end of the 18-month grant period, we'll publish a full open-source toolkit so any government can deploy this without starting from scratch.

Who We Are

FinMango is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit that's operated in 13+ countries, building tools that solve the navigation gap between people and the financial programs they need. FreshFind is our flagship project, supported by Google.org's AI for Government Innovation Impact Challenge.
