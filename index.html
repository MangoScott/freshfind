01

the problem

Picture a grandmother in rural Kentucky. She qualifies for SNAP, WIC referrals, and food pantry access. She doesn't know about any of them. Not because the information doesn't exist, but because finding it requires navigating a maze of federal, state, and local systems that weren't designed to talk to each other.

She's not alone. 47 million Americans are food insecure right now. Millions of them qualify for benefits they'll never receive. The programs exist. The funding exists. But the path from "you're eligible" to "you're enrolled" is so fragmented that people just... give up.

That's not a knowledge problem. It's a navigation problem. And it's the problem we built FreshFind to solve.

We spent two years researching food insecurity in five very different U.S. communities. The same pattern kept showing up everywhere: the programs are there, but the barriers to actually accessing them are enormous. In Illinois, half of eligible elderly residents don't receive SNAP. Half. In Wolfe County, Kentucky, food pantries provide 1 meal for every 9 that SNAP delivers. The county's food insecurity rate is 27.3%.

And it's getting worse. The 2025 House Budget Plan introduced the largest proposed SNAP cuts in history and expanded work requirements to age 64. That puts 135,000 more Kentuckians at risk of losing benefits. The people most affected by these changes are the ones with the fewest resources to figure out what the changes even mean for them.

02

the solution

Think of FreshFind as the caseworker you wish existed. But one that's available 24/7, knows every program in your area, and can walk you through the entire process from "do I qualify?" to "your application is submitted."

We built it on top of tools we already had in the field. It works with local governments, not around them. And it does four specific things:

layer one

it figures out what you qualify for

You answer a few questions. FreshFind checks everything at once: SNAP, WIC, free school meals, food pantries, utility assistance, Medicaid nutrition programs. Instead of applying to six different places and hoping for the best, you get one clear picture of every resource available to you.

✦ we already built this — it's live right now
layer two

it finds actual food near you

Knowing you qualify doesn't put groceries on the table. So FreshFind goes further: it finds the nearest stores that accept SNAP and actually carry fresh produce, pulls up food pantry hours, figures out how you'd get there, and checks what's in season. All based on your exact location.

✦ we already built this too — also live
layer three

it helps you actually apply

This is where the real impact happens. Most people drop off during the application process because it's confusing, the forms are long, and nobody tells you what documents you need until you've already waited in line. FreshFind pre-fills what it can, walks you through the rest, flags deadlines, and sends reminders when it's time to recertify. Caseworkers spend less time on paperwork; families actually get enrolled.

layer four

it watches for policy changes so you don't have to

Policy changes constantly. New work requirements, benefit reductions, rule changes at the state level. Most people find out they've lost benefits after it happens. FreshFind monitors all of it and proactively reaches out to affected households: here's what changed, here's what it means for you, here's what to do next.

already built

this isn't a pitch deck — we've already started

The first two layers? They exist. The Food Assistance Calculator is live on our site right now — it checks SNAP eligibility, estimates your benefits, and connects you to nearby resources. The Food Desert Analyzer scores grocery access, fresh food availability, and transportation barriers for any zip code. FreshFind takes these tools and adds AI on top to make the whole experience feel like talking to someone who actually knows the system.

03

research foundation

We didn't just read about food insecurity. We went to five very different places and mapped what was actually happening on the ground: who's going hungry, why the existing programs aren't reaching them, what's blocking access. That research is what FreshFind's AI actually runs on.

Chicago, IL
~500k food insecure residents. 1.88M SNAP recipients with $300M distributed monthly. Every $1 returns $1.54 GDP. Food Depository submitted 21k benefit applications since 2020.
Atlanta, GA
13.7% food insecurity rate. Only 38% of SNAP retailers in majority-Black neighborhoods carry produce vs. 75% elsewhere. Highest wealth inequality in the nation.
Kansas City, MO
~79k food insecure adults. Strong nonprofit ecosystem but limited government initiatives. Min wage disparity between KCMO and KCKS creates economic divide.
Twin Falls, ID
~10% food insecurity rate driven by structural access barriers, not supply. SNAP retailers mostly convenience stores. Bus system replaced by smartphone-only microtransit.
Wolfe Co., KY
27.3% food insecurity — highest in Kentucky. 38% poverty rate. 4 food stores in entire county. 135k Kentuckians at risk of losing SNAP. $1.1M annual food budget shortfall.
04

government partnership

We don't ask people to download another app or visit another website. FreshFind plugs into places where people are already showing up. The goal is to meet folks where they are, not where we wish they were.

health departments

Families are already there for WIC and nutrition visits. Why not check everything else while they're at it?

public libraries

In rural areas especially, the library is the one place everyone trusts and everyone can get to

school districts

If a kid qualifies for free lunch, the family probably qualifies for a lot more

211 centers

People already call 211 for help. FreshFind makes that help more specific and more complete

Here's what government partners actually get: fewer incomplete applications clogging the system, more people enrolled, and real data on what their community actually needs. Caseworkers spend less time on intake and more time on the work that requires a human.

05

how we'll know it's working

enrollment
Are more eligible people actually getting enrolled? We'll measure SNAP and WIC enrollment before and after FreshFind launches in each community.
access
How much faster do people go from "I might qualify" to actually receiving benefits? And are they getting connected to stores that sell real food, not just the nearest gas station?
efficiency
Fewer abandoned applications. Less time per enrollment for caseworkers. More people successfully recertifying instead of accidentally losing benefits.
economic
Every dollar of SNAP benefits generates $1.54 in GDP. So when we help someone enroll, we're not just feeding a family — we're putting money into the local economy. We'll track total benefit dollars unlocked.
06

can this work everywhere?

That's the question we asked ourselves before we started. So we deliberately picked five communities that have almost nothing in common: a major metro (Chicago), a high-inequality city (Atlanta), a mid-size metro (Kansas City), a growing rural hub (Twin Falls), and a deep-rural Appalachian county (Wolfe County, KY). The same approach worked in all of them.

Here's why it scales: each time we add a community, we feed FreshFind the local data — which stores accept SNAP, where the food pantries are, how transit works, what the state rules are. The AI builds a local model on top of the same core platform. We don't rebuild anything. We just teach it a new place.

And eventually, this goes beyond the U.S. FinMango already operates in 13+ countries, and the navigation problem is the same everywhere. India's Public Distribution System, Brazil's Bolsa Família — different programs, same frustration. People who need help can't find it.

07

about FinMango

I founded FinMango in 2017 as a student at Kent State. The original idea was simple: build free tools that help people navigate financial systems that weren't designed with them in mind. That idea turned into a 501(c)(3) nonprofit that's now reached over 1 million people in 13+ countries.

Along the way, we started publishing original research on financial inequality, built the Food Assistance Calculator and Food Desert Analyzer, and grew a network of student ambassadors. We've partnered with Google Health, the World Health Organization, the World Bank, and the IMF. But the thing I keep coming back to is this: the tools are only useful if people can actually find and use them.

FreshFind is the most ambitious thing we've ever tried to build. It takes everything we've learned — the research, the tools, the community relationships — and puts AI to work on the specific problem of getting food assistance to people who need it. Not studying the problem. Solving it.

08

funding & timeline

We're asking Google.org for $2M to take FreshFind from working prototypes to a deployed, scaled product over 18 months. Here's the plan:

build
months 1–6
Take our existing tools and add the AI layer. Build the unified eligibility engine that checks SNAP, WIC, and local programs all at once. Connect resource routing to live produce availability data. Get our first government partner signed and onboarded.
deploy
months 7–12
Launch in 2–3 partner communities. Get the Application Assistant working so people can actually submit applications through FreshFind. Install it in health departments, libraries, school districts. Test it with real users and fix what doesn't work.
scale
months 13–18
Turn on policy monitoring so we can proactively alert households when rules change. Expand to 5+ additional communities. Publish everything we've learned — what worked, what didn't — and release the toolkit so others can replicate it.

Billions in food assistance goes unclaimed every year. Not because people don't need it. Because finding it is just too hard. We can fix that.
