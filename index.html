01

the problem

47 million Americans are food insecure. 42 million receive SNAP benefits monthly. But millions more qualify and never receive them.

The gap between eligibility and enrollment is not a knowledge problem — it's a navigation problem. Applying for food assistance requires navigating fragmented federal, state, and local systems, each with different eligibility rules, documentation requirements, and application processes.

For a single mother in Atlanta's south side, a seasonal worker in Twin Falls, or an elderly resident in rural Wolfe County, Kentucky, the complexity alone is a barrier.

FinMango's research across five diverse U.S. communities reveals a consistent pattern: food insecurity is driven less by the absence of programs and more by structural barriers to accessing them. In Illinois, only 50% of eligible elderly residents receive SNAP. In Kentucky's Wolfe County — the state's most food-insecure county at 27.3% — food pantries provide just 1 meal for every 9 that SNAP delivers.

Meanwhile, policy is shifting rapidly. The 2025 House Budget Plan introduced the largest proposed cuts to SNAP in history, expanded work requirements to age 64, and placed 135,000 additional Kentuckians at risk of losing benefits. The people most affected are the least equipped to navigate the changing landscape.

02

the solution

FreshFind is an AI-powered food assistance navigator that transforms how people discover, qualify for, and access food resources — serving as a 24/7 caseworker that guides users from awareness to enrollment to ongoing support.

Built by FinMango and deployed in partnership with local governments, FreshFind uses generative and agentic AI across four integrated layers:

layer one

smart eligibility navigator

Users answer a short series of conversational questions. FreshFind cross-references eligibility across multiple programs simultaneously — SNAP, WIC, free school meals, food pantries, utility assistance, Medicaid nutrition programs — delivering a single, unified view of every resource they qualify for.

✦ live prototype: Food Assistance Calculator
layer two

agentic resource router

FreshFind doesn't stop at "you qualify." The agent locates the nearest SNAP-accepting retailers that carry fresh produce, identifies food pantry hours, maps transportation options, and surfaces seasonal availability — personalized to each user's exact location.

✦ live prototype: Food Desert Analyzer
layer three

application assistant

The highest-impact layer. FreshFind helps users complete benefit applications by pre-filling forms, walking through documentation requirements, flagging deadlines, and sending recertification reminders — directly closing the enrollment gap while reducing caseworker burden.

layer four

policy intelligence engine

FreshFind monitors federal, state, and local policy changes in real time and proactively notifies affected users. When eligibility changes or benefit reductions take effect, the system alerts households and helps them navigate the transition. No community is caught off guard.

already built

we're not starting from zero

FinMango has already built and deployed working prototypes of FreshFind's first two layers. The Food Assistance Calculator determines SNAP eligibility, estimates benefits, and connects users to nearby resources. The Food Desert Analyzer scores grocery store access, fresh food availability, and transportation barriers by zip code. FreshFind adds agentic AI on top of this proven infrastructure.

03

research foundation

FreshFind is grounded in original research across five diverse U.S. communities. Each case study maps the local food insecurity landscape, SNAP enrollment patterns, nonprofit capacity, transportation barriers, and policy environment — providing the data infrastructure that powers FreshFind's AI.

Chicago, IL
~500k food insecure residents. 1.88M SNAP recipients with $300M distributed monthly. Every $1 returns $1.54 GDP. Food Depository submitted 21k benefit applications since 2020.
Atlanta, GA
13.7% food insecurity rate. Only 38% of SNAP retailers in majority-Black neighborhoods carry produce vs. 75% elsewhere. Highest wealth inequality in the nation.
Kansas City, MO
~79k food insecure adults. Strong nonprofit ecosystem but limited government initiatives. Min wage disparity between KCMO and KCKS creates economic divide.
Twin Falls, ID
~10% food insecurity rate driven by structural access barriers, not supply. SNAP retailers mostly convenience stores. Bus system replaced by smartphone-only microtransit.
Wolfe Co., KY
27.3% food insecurity — highest in Kentucky. 38% poverty rate. 4 food stores in entire county. 135k Kentuckians at risk of losing SNAP. $1.1M annual food budget shortfall.
04

government partnership

FreshFind deploys through existing government infrastructure — not as a replacement, but as a force multiplier. Local governments embed FreshFind into touchpoints where food-insecure residents already interact with public services.

health departments

WIC offices and clinics where families already seek nutrition support

public libraries

Trusted physical access points, especially critical in rural areas

school districts

Reaching families through free/reduced meal enrollment touchpoints

211 centers

Integrating into existing social service referral infrastructure

For government partners, FreshFind reduces caseworker burden, increases benefit enrollment rates, and provides real-time data on community needs — giving agencies actionable intelligence to improve service delivery.

06

scalability

Five case studies — spanning a major metro, a mid-size city, a growing rural hub, a high-inequality urban center, and a deep-rural Appalachian county — demonstrate that FreshFind adapts across demographic, geographic, and political contexts.

Each new community requires a localized knowledge base: SNAP retailer maps, food pantry networks, transportation infrastructure, and policy rules. FreshFind's AI ingests these layers and generates a community-specific resource model, making expansion to any U.S. county achievable without rebuilding the platform.

Long-term, the model extends internationally. FinMango already operates across 13+ countries, and food assistance navigation challenges are universal — from India's Public Distribution System to Brazil's Bolsa Família.

07

about FinMango

FinMango is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit founded in 2017 with a mission to solve complex problems that endanger the financial health of young adults and vulnerable communities. What began as a classroom idea at Kent State University has grown into a global organization impacting over 1 million people across 13+ countries.

The organization operates at the intersection of research, education, and advocacy — publishing original research on financial inequality, building free digital tools (including the Food Assistance Calculator and Food Desert Analyzer), and empowering a global network of student ambassadors. Partnerships include Google Health, the World Health Organization, the World Bank, and the International Monetary Fund.

FreshFind represents FinMango's most ambitious initiative: applying agentic AI to the organization's deep research foundation and existing tool infrastructure to directly close the gap between food-insecure people and the resources they need.
