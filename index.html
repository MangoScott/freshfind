01

the gap nobody is filling

Getting approved for SNAP doesn't feed anyone. A SNAP card in a food desert is a card with nowhere to go. And when policy changes hit, the people most affected are always the last to hear.

Investment has rightly gone toward getting people enrolled. That matters. But enrollment is only half the problem. The pipeline ends at "you have a card." The actual problem continues: Where do you buy food? Can you get there? Does the store near you actually carry produce, or is it a gas station that accepts EBT? What happens when policy changes yank coverage without warning?

Atlanta, GA
Only 38% of SNAP retailers in majority-Black neighborhoods carry fresh produce. In other neighborhoods, it's 75%.
Twin Falls, ID
The bus system went smartphone-only. People who can't use the app are stranded. Most SNAP retailers are convenience stores.
Wolfe County, KY
4 food stores in the entire county. Food insecurity rate: 27.3%. Poverty rate: 38%. The nearest real grocery is 12 miles away.
Nationally
$140B+ in federal benefits go unclaimed every year. Not because people don't qualify. Because the systems are too fragmented to navigate.

The programs exist. The funding exists. The path between them is broken. And every existing tool waits for people to show up and ask for help. Nobody is going to them first.

how freshfind works the centerpiece

FreshFind is a four-stage autonomous AI system. Each stage triggers the next without human intervention. The loop runs continuously. No one has to ask for help. The system finds them first.

Detect
Agentic AI — Policy Monitoring

The system reads the Federal Register every day. It monitors state SNAP rule changes, disaster declarations, benefit recertification schedules, and FinMango's Financial Health Barometer, which tracks financial stress signals across all 50 states from BLS, Census, HUD, FRED, and Google Trends data.

When SNAP work requirements expand to age 64, or a state changes its income thresholds, or a disaster activates D-SNAP, the system doesn't wait for someone to notice. It parses the change, maps it to affected programs, and triggers the next stage automatically. This is genuine agentic behavior: the system watches, reasons about policy changes, and initiates action on its own.

Identify
Predictive AI — Population Targeting

The AI cross-references detected policy changes with community-level data: FinMango's 2-year UVA field research across 5 communities, Census demographics, USDA food access data, and state-specific eligibility rules.

Not "135,000 Kentuckians" as a headline stat. Specific clusters: these zip codes in eastern Kentucky, where 60% of SNAP recipients are ages 55-64, where the nearest grocery store is 12 miles away, where transit doesn't exist. That's the precision that makes proactive outreach work.

Reach
Generative AI — Personalized Outreach

The system generates personalized, plain-language notifications delivered through government channels people already use. Not cold-contact marketing. The state agency sends it under their own name, through their existing communication channels.

Each message adapts to the household's location, family composition, program history, and local food access conditions. "The rules changed. Here's what it means for you. Here are 3 programs you still qualify for. Here's where to buy groceries near you that accept EBT and carry fresh produce. Here's how to apply."

Navigate
AI Decision Engine — Not a Chatbot

FreshFind is not a chatbot. Not a "conversational AI." A stressed, overwhelmed person who just lost their benefits doesn't need to figure out how to prompt an AI. They need: "answer these 5 questions, here's your personalized plan."

1
Guided intake. 5-8 structured questions. Household size, location, income, current programs. Clean, simple, fast.
2
Personalized dashboard. Everything you qualify for, in one view. Every relevant program across federal, state, and local levels.
3
Application assistance. Pre-filled forms. Document checklists that tell you exactly what to bring. Deadline tracking with reminders.
4
Food access map. Stores accepting EBT that carry fresh produce. Pantry hours. Transit routes. What's in season nearby.
5
Ongoing alerts. Text or email when something changes: new program available, recertification due, new store nearby, policy change affecting your benefits.

Then it stays. Disaster hits? The system automatically activates D-SNAP, emergency food resources, and disaster-specific programs for affected zip codes. New legislation? The loop restarts at Stage 1. This is a full agentic loop. Continuous, autonomous, proactive.

What the AI does (backend)
◆ Monitors policy feeds autonomously
◆ Identifies affected populations by zip code
◆ Generates personalized outreach at scale
◆ Cross-references food access data
◆ Tracks deadlines and eligibility changes
What the user sees (frontend)
→ 5 simple questions
→ "Here's what you qualify for"
→ Pre-filled applications
→ Map: stores, pantries, transit
→ Text alerts when things change

This distinction matters for two audiences. Google.org reviewers have seen 500 chatbot pitches. A backend agentic system with a structured frontend is architecturally different and more credible. And a state SNAP director will worry about chatbot hallucinations and liability. A structured decision engine with deterministic outputs from verified program data is something they can actually approve and deploy.

04

built on real data not assumptions

In Collaboration with the University of Virginia

We spent two years in five communities mapping what's actually happening on the ground: who's going hungry, why existing programs aren't reaching them, what's blocking access. This research is what FreshFind's AI runs on. No competitor has this data in the food access space.

Chicago, IL
~500k food insecure residents. 1.88M SNAP recipients with $300M distributed monthly. Every $1 returns $1.54 GDP. Food Depository submitted 21k benefit applications since 2020.
Atlanta, GA
13.7% food insecurity rate. Only 38% of SNAP retailers in majority-Black neighborhoods carry produce vs. 75% elsewhere. Highest wealth inequality in the nation.
Kansas City, MO
~79k food insecure adults. Strong nonprofit ecosystem but limited government initiatives. Min wage disparity between KCMO and KCKS creates economic divide.
Twin Falls, ID
~10% food insecurity rate driven by structural access barriers, not supply. SNAP retailers mostly convenience stores. Bus system replaced by smartphone-only microtransit.
Wolfe Co., KY
27.3% food insecurity — highest in Kentucky. 38% poverty rate. 4 food stores in entire county. 135k Kentuckians at risk of losing SNAP under H.R. 1. $1.1M annual food budget shortfall.
05

how it reaches people

FreshFind never faces the public as an unknown brand. It always shows up through an institution people already trust. The government partner owns the relationship. FreshFind powers what's behind it.

Primary Partnership

State SNAP & Medicaid Agencies

The state agency already has enrollment rolls with contact info for every SNAP and Medicaid household. When policy changes hit, they're legally required to notify affected households. Most states have no automated system to do this at scale. FreshFind is that system.

The agency shares permissioned household data through a secure pipeline. FreshFind identifies affected households, generates personalized outreach content. The agency sends it under their own name through their existing channels. Respondents land in FreshFind's Navigate experience. The agency gets outcome reporting: how many people were reached, how many enrolled in alternative programs, how many accessed food resources.

211 Call Centers

Someone calls 211 asking for food help. This happens millions of times a year. The 211 operator has FreshFind open on their screen as a real-time support tool. They enter the caller's zip code and basic household info. Instant cross-program eligibility results, a map of nearby stores that accept EBT and carry fresh produce, food pantry hours, and transit options. The 211 center stays the entry point. FreshFind provides the depth.

Health Departments

A family comes in for a WIC appointment, immunization, or nutrition counseling. They're already in the building. The caseworker runs a 2-minute FreshFind screening during the visit. "You're here for WIC, but you also qualify for SNAP, there's a food pantry with Saturday hours 3 miles from your house, and you're eligible for utility assistance you're not receiving." One visit, complete picture. Family leaves with a printed or texted summary of everything they qualify for.

School Districts

The district already knows which kids get free or reduced-price lunch. That data is a strong signal that the family qualifies for more. FreshFind cross-references the district's free lunch data with broader program eligibility rules. It generates personalized outreach for each family, sent through the school's existing parent communication system. "Based on your child's enrollment, your family may qualify for 3 additional programs." The school is the trusted messenger. FreshFind is the intelligence behind the message.

Public Libraries

In rural areas especially, the library is often the only public building with internet, AC, and trusted staff. FreshFind runs on library computer terminals as a kiosk-style interface. Someone walks in, sits down, and the guided intake walks them through everything. Structured questions, personalized results, food routing, document checklists, application help. Library staff can assist but don't need to be experts in benefit programs.

What Government Partners Get

1
A tool to fulfill notification mandates they can't currently handle at scale. H.R. 1 requires states to notify affected households. Most states have no automated system for this.
2
Fewer incomplete applications clogging caseworker queues. Pre-screened, informed applicants with correct documentation arrive ready.
3
Real data on food access gaps in their jurisdiction. Not just enrollment numbers, but whether enrolled people can actually buy groceries.
4
Scalable surge capacity. When a disaster hits or a major policy change takes effect, FreshFind absorbs the spike that would otherwise overwhelm caseworkers and call centers.
5
No new infrastructure required. FreshFind integrates with existing systems and communication channels. The agency doesn't need to build, buy, or maintain new technology.

Data & Security: FreshFind doesn't sell or retain user data. Built for HIPAA/PII compliance with government-grade security standards. Integrates securely with existing government systems through compliant data pipelines.

06

how we'll measure it

food access
Are enrolled people actually able to buy fresh groceries? Food desert score improvements in pilot communities. Percentage of SNAP households within 1 mile of a store that carries produce.
proactive reach
Percentage of affected households contacted before policy changes take effect. Time from policy change detection to household notification. Target: under 72 hours.
benefits unlocked
Total dollars in benefits unlocked across all programs. Application completion rates through FreshFind vs. baseline. Cross-program enrollment (households connected to 2+ programs they weren't previously receiving).
efficiency
Caseworker time saved per enrollment. Reduction in incomplete applications. Higher recertification rates. Fewer people losing benefits due to missed deadlines.
07

built to replicate

FreshFind is a toolkit, not a custom project. Any state supplies local data: which stores accept SNAP, food pantry locations, transit routes, state eligibility rules. The same core AI infrastructure deploys. No rebuilding. No new codebase. The scaling unit is the government partnership, not the individual user. One government's success becomes another government's starting point.

When a presidential disaster declaration drops, FreshFind automatically activates D-SNAP, emergency food resources, and crisis programs for affected zip codes. That's an automatic scaling trigger that fires 60+ times per year in the U.S.

The model travels. FinMango already operates in 13+ countries. India's Public Distribution System, Brazil's Bolsa Família, EU food assistance programs. The government navigation problem is universal. At the end of the 18-month grant period, we'll publish a full open-source toolkit so any city, state, or national government can deploy this without starting from scratch.

08

about FinMango

Founded in 2017 at Kent State. The original idea: build free tools that help people navigate financial systems that weren't designed with them in mind. That became a 501(c)(3) nonprofit that's reached over 1 million people in 13+ countries.

Partnered with Google Health, WHO, World Bank, and IMF. Published original research on financial inequality. TEDx speaker (Brazil, Togo). 30+ public GitHub repos. Open source everything. The thread through everything: the navigation problem. People are entitled to help that exists, but the systems are so fragmented that millions never access it. FreshFind is the most ambitious thing this organization has ever tried to build.

the roadmap

$1.5M over 18 months. From working prototypes to deployed, scaled infrastructure.

build
months 1–6
Secure HIPAA/PII-compliant AI infrastructure. Build the policy monitoring engine (Stage 1) and population targeting layer (Stage 2). Complete compliance audits. Onboard 2-3 government partners from the communities where the UVA research was conducted.
deploy
months 7–12
Launch the proactive outreach system (Stage 3) with pilot partners. Deploy the full Navigate experience (Stage 4) through health departments, libraries, and 211 centers. Test against real policy changes with real households and caseworkers. Iterate based on live feedback.
scale
months 13–18
Expand to 5+ state partners. Activate disaster-mode for new declarations. Publish open-source toolkit and impact report for national replication. Package the system for international deployment.
